What is an NDA?

A Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) is a legal contract that protects confidential information from being shared with unauthorized parties.

By signing an NDA, the recipient agrees not to disclose any confidential information they have access to during the course of their work or business relationship with the disclosing party.

When could I breach an NDA?

Sticking with your NDA might seem simple: just don't talk about it. It's not always so straightforward, however, and you may encounter situations where there's pressure to share information that would break your NDA or where you're discussing information prohibited by your NDA, but it isn't obvious.
